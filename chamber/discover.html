Mendoza the land of the good sun

The Province of Mendoza is located in the central west of the Argentine Republic, at the foot of Cerro Aconcagua, the highest peak in the Andes Mountains. Mendoza has been and is a Land of Opportunities.

Strategic location

Mendoza is located on the line of shortest distance between the oceanic coastlines that passes through the great southern capitals. A thousand kilometers separate it from Buenos Aires; four hundred from Santiago de Chile and three thousand two hundred from San Pablo.

Mendoza Urban Population

The City of Mendoza with its urban agglomeration, Greater Mendoza, is the fourth largest population unit in the country with almost 1,100,000 inhabitants and 848,660 inhabitants

The Puente del Inca natural monument is a protected natural area in the province of Mendoza in Argentina, declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO

The Cristo Redentor tunnel, officially designated Los Libertadores International Pass, is an automotive border crossing that connects Argentina and Chile through the Andes mountain range, between the province of Mendoza, Argentina, and the Region of Valparaíso, Chile.
